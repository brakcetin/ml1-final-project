%% --- ABSTRACT ---
\begin{abstract}
This report presents a comparative study of four distinct machine learning pipelines for Human Activity Recognition (HAR) using the UCI Smartphone dataset. We investigated the trade-offs between computational efficiency, predictive accuracy, and generalization capability across different methodologies. 

Our experimental results demonstrate that the \textbf{Baseline Approach} (utilizing all 561 features) achieved the highest test accuracy of \textbf{95.7\%}. In contrast, the \textbf{PCA-based Approach} successfully reduced the feature space by \textbf{82\%} (102 components) while maintaining a competitive accuracy of \textbf{93.32\%}, proving its suitability for resource-constrained embedded systems. Furthermore, the \textbf{Subject-Disjoint Approach} highlighted the critical challenge of generalization, revealing a performance gap between known subjects (98.3\%) and unseen subjects (94.7\%). We conclude that while full-feature models offer maximum accuracy, dimensionality reduction combined with ensemble learning provides an optimal balance for real-world deployment.
\end{abstract}
